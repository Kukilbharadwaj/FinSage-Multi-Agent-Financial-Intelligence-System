INSURANCE AND LOAN RULES IN INDIA
====================================

TERM INSURANCE
---------------
What: Pure life cover with no investment/savings component.
Purpose: Financial protection for family in case of policyholder's death.

Key Rules:
- Coverage: 10-15x annual income (e.g., ₹15 LPA salary → ₹1.5 to ₹2 Cr cover)
- Premium: ₹500-1,000/month for ₹1 Cr cover at age 25-30 (non-smoker)
- Buying online gives 30-50% lower premium than offline
- Claim Settlement Ratio: Choose insurer with >95% CSR
- Top insurers: HDFC Life, ICICI Prudential, Max Life, Tata AIA, LIC
- Tax benefit: Premium deductible under Section 80C (up to ₹1.5 lakh)
- Maturity: No payout if policyholder survives — that's why it's cheapest
- Riders: Add critical illness, accidental death rider for extra protection

DO NOT BUY:
- ULIPs (Unit Linked Insurance Plans) — poor returns, high charges
- Endowment plans — returns of 4-5% are below inflation
- Money-back policies — worst of both insurance and investment
- Rule: Keep insurance and investment SEPARATE

HEALTH INSURANCE
-----------------
What: Covers hospitalization expenses, surgeries, and treatments.

Key Rules:
- Minimum cover: ₹5 lakh individual, ₹10 lakh family floater
- Super top-up: Add ₹25-50 lakh super top-up at low premium for major illnesses
- Pre-existing diseases: Covered after 2-4 year waiting period
- No Claim Bonus: Cover increases 10-50% for each claim-free year
- Network hospitals: Cashless treatment at network hospitals
- Section 80D deduction:
  - Self/spouse/children (below 60): Up to ₹25,000
  - Self (senior citizen 60+): Up to ₹50,000
  - Parents (below 60): Additional ₹25,000
  - Parents (60+): Additional ₹50,000
  - Maximum total: ₹1,00,000 per year
  - Preventive health check-up: ₹5,000 included within above limits
- Corporate insurance is NOT enough: Buy personal cover separately
- Buy early: Premium increases with age, pre-existing conditions may cause rejection

HOME LOAN RULES
-----------------
Interest Rates (2024-25):
- SBI: 8.50-9.65% p.a.
- HDFC: 8.75-9.65% p.a.
- ICICI: 8.75-9.85% p.a.
- Rates linked to repo rate (RBI sets this)

Tax Benefits:
- Section 24(b): Interest paid on home loan — up to ₹2,00,000 deduction (self-occupied property)
- Section 80C: Principal repayment — up to ₹1,50,000 deduction (within 80C overall limit)
- Section 80EEA: Additional ₹1,50,000 interest deduction for first-time home buyers (stamp duty value up to ₹45 lakh) — available under old regime only
- For let-out property: Entire interest amount deductible with no upper limit

EMI Calculation Formula:
- EMI = [P × R × (1+R)^N] / [(1+R)^N – 1]
- P = Principal loan amount, R = Monthly interest rate, N = Number of months
- Example: ₹50 lakh loan, 8.5% for 20 years → EMI ≈ ₹43,391

Tips:
- Choose floating rate (not fixed) — historically cheaper over long term
- Make prepayments whenever possible — saves lakhs in interest
- Keep EMI below 40% of monthly income
- Tenure: 15-20 years is optimal (avoid 30 years — too much interest)

EDUCATION LOAN
-----------------
Tax Benefit — Section 80E:
- Interest paid on education loan is fully deductible (NO upper limit)
- Available for 8 years from the year of first repayment
- Loan must be for higher education (in India or abroad)
- Available for self, spouse, or children
- Available in BOTH old and new tax regimes

PERSONAL LOAN
--------------
- No tax benefit (since it's unsecured)
- Interest rates: 10-24% p.a. (much higher than home/education loan)
- Avoid personal loans for investments — high interest eats returns
- Use only for genuine emergencies if no emergency fund available

CAR LOAN
----------
- No tax benefit for salaried individuals
- Interest rates: 7.5-12% p.a.
- Down payment: Min 10-20% of car price
- Tip: Always negotiate interest rate — banks have room to reduce
- Rule: Car EMI should not exceed 10% of monthly income

CIBIL SCORE (CREDIT SCORE)
============================
- Range: 300-900
- 750+: Good score — best loan rates and approval chances
- 650-750: Average — may get loans at higher interest rates
- Below 650: Poor — loan rejection likely
- Factors affecting score: Payment history (35%), credit utilization (30%), credit age (15%), credit mix (10%), enquiries (10%)
- Tips to improve:
  1. Pay all EMIs and credit card bills on time
  2. Keep credit utilization below 30% of limit
  3. Don't apply for multiple loans/cards in short period
  4. Maintain old credit card accounts (credit age matters)
  5. Check CIBIL report annually for errors — free at cibil.com
