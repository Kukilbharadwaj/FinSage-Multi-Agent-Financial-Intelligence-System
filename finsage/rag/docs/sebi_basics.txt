SEBI INVESTOR CHARTER — RIGHTS AND RESPONSIBILITIES
-----------------------------------------------------
The Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) is the regulatory authority for securities
markets in India, established under the SEBI Act, 1992.

Investor Rights:
1. Right to receive investment advice only from SEBI-registered entities
2. Right to full disclosure of risks associated with any investment
3. Right to timely settlement of trades (T+1 for equity)
4. Right to access grievance redressal through SCORES (SEBI Complaints Redress System)
5. Right to receive contract notes and statements from brokers
6. Right to data privacy and protection of personal financial information

Investor Responsibilities:
1. Deal only with SEBI-registered intermediaries (brokers, advisors, mutual funds)
2. Read all scheme documents carefully before investing
3. Verify credentials of advisors and brokers through SEBI website
4. Report suspicious activities or fraud to SEBI
5. Keep records of all transactions and communications
6. Understand that investments in securities are subject to market risks

SEBI-REGISTERED INVESTMENT ADVISOR (RIA)
==========================================
What is an RIA:
- An individual or firm registered with SEBI under the SEBI (Investment Advisers) Regulations, 2013
- Must pass NISM Series X-A and X-B certification exams
- Must maintain minimum net worth (₹5 lakh for individuals, ₹50 lakh for firms)
- Can charge fees (flat fee or AUM-based) but CANNOT earn commissions
- Must act as a fiduciary — client's interest comes first

Why it matters:
- Unregistered advisors on social media, YouTube, or Telegram are NOT regulated
- Tip providers on WhatsApp groups are likely unregistered and potentially illegal
- Stock recommendations from TV anchors are NOT personalized advice
- AI-generated financial information (like this system) is NOT investment advice
- Only a SEBI-registered RIA or mutual fund distributor can give personalized recommendations

RISK DISCLOSURE FOR EQUITY INVESTMENTS
========================================

General Risks:
- Market Risk: Stock prices can fall significantly and you may lose part or all of your investment
- Volatility Risk: Indian markets can be highly volatile, especially mid-cap and small-cap stocks
- Liquidity Risk: Some stocks may have low trading volume, making it hard to sell quickly
- Concentration Risk: Investing too much in a single stock or sector increases risk
- Currency Risk: For international investments, rupee depreciation/appreciation affects returns
- Regulatory Risk: Government policy changes (tax rules, FDI limits) can impact returns
- Interest Rate Risk: RBI rate changes affect equity valuations and debt fund NAVs
- Credit Risk: Applicable to debt investments — issuer may default

Equity-Specific Risks:
- No guaranteed returns — equity is not a fixed income instrument
- Short-term trading is essentially speculation and carries high risk of loss
- Intraday trading results in losses for over 90% of retail traders (SEBI study)
- F&O trading: 9 out of 10 individual traders in F&O segment lose money (SEBI report 2023)
- IPO investments carry listing risk and may trade below issue price

Risk Mitigation Strategies:
1. Diversification: Spread investments across sectors and market caps
2. SIP mode: Invest regularly to average out volatility
3. Stop-loss: Always set a stop-loss for trading positions
4. Research: Study fundamentals before buying any stock
5. Long-term horizon: Equity rewards patience (5-10+ year holding)
6. Asset allocation: Mix equity, debt, gold based on age and risk tolerance
7. Never invest borrowed money in equity
