TRADING RULES IN INDIA — INTRADAY, F&O, OPTIONS
===================================================

INTRADAY TRADING RULES
========================
What: Buy and sell same stock on same day. No delivery — position squared off by 3:15 PM.
Margin: Brokers provide 5-20x leverage for intraday (MIS/BO/CO orders).
Brokerage: Typically ₹20/order flat (Zerodha, Groww) or percentage-based.

SEBI RULES FOR INTRADAY:
- Peak margin reporting: Broker must collect upfront margin from client
- Margin penalty: If client uses more margin than available, exchange levies penalty
- Settlement: T+1 settlement for equities (same-day for intraday, no delivery)
- Short selling allowed in intraday — sell first, buy later to cover

INTRADAY TRADING STRATEGIES:
1. Opening Range Breakout (ORB): Trade breakout of first 15-30 min range
2. VWAP Strategy: Buy below VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price), sell above
3. Moving Average Crossover: 5 EMA crossing 20 EMA for entry
4. Support/Resistance: Trade bounces off key S/R levels
5. Gap Up/Down Strategy: Trade gap fills — stocks tend to fill gaps

INTRADAY RISK MANAGEMENT:
- Risk max 1-2% of capital per trade
- Use strict stop-loss — NEVER trade without stop-loss
- Risk:Reward ratio minimum 1:2 (risk ₹100 to make ₹200)
- Avoid trading first 15 minutes (volatile opening)
- Max 2-3 trades per day for beginners
- SEBI data: 90% of intraday traders lose money — be warned

INTRADAY TAXATION:
- Profits taxed as business income (speculative) at slab rate
- If you do intraday regularly, file ITR-3 (not ITR-1)
- Intraday losses can ONLY offset intraday profits (speculative loss)
- Cannot offset against delivery-based STCG/LTCG or salary income
- STT paid: 0.025% on sell side only

F&O (FUTURES AND OPTIONS) TRADING
====================================

FUTURES:
- Agreement to buy/sell asset at predetermined price on future date
- Lot size: Fixed by exchange (e.g., Nifty 50 lot = 25 units, BankNifty lot = 15 units)
- Margin: SPAN + Exposure margin required (typically 10-15% of contract value)
- Mark-to-Market (MTM): Daily profit/loss settlement
- Expiry: Last Thursday of every month (weekly expiry every Thursday for index)
- Rollover: Move position to next month expiry before current expiry

OPTIONS:
- Right (not obligation) to buy (Call) or sell (Put) at a specific price
- Call Option (CE): Right to buy — bullish bet
- Put Option (PE): Right to sell — bearish bet
- Premium: Price paid to buy an option
- Strike Price: The price at which option can be exercised
- ITM (In The Money): Call strike < current price, Put strike > current price
- ATM (At The Money): Strike price ≈ current price
- OTM (Out of The Money): Call strike > current price, Put strike < current price

OPTIONS GREEKS:
- Delta: How much option price changes per ₹1 move in underlying (0 to 1 for calls, 0 to -1 for puts)
- Gamma: Rate of change of delta — highest for ATM options
- Theta: Time decay — how much value option loses per day. Option sellers benefit from theta.
- Vega: Sensitivity to implied volatility changes
- Rule: Option buyers need big moves to profit. Option sellers profit from time decay.

POPULAR OPTIONS STRATEGIES:
1. Long Call: Buy call when bullish. Max loss = premium paid.
2. Long Put: Buy put when bearish. Max loss = premium paid.
3. Covered Call: Own stock + sell OTM call. Income strategy.
4. Protective Put: Own stock + buy put. Insurance strategy.
5. Bull Call Spread: Buy ATM call + sell OTM call. Limited risk/reward.
6. Bear Put Spread: Buy ATM put + sell OTM put. Bearish with limited risk.
7. Iron Condor: Sell OTM call + sell OTM put + buy further OTM call + buy further OTM put. Range-bound strategy.
8. Straddle: Buy ATM call + ATM put. Profit from big move in either direction.
9. Strangle: Buy OTM call + OTM put. Cheaper than straddle, needs bigger move.

F&O TAXATION (INDIA):
- F&O profits: Non-speculative business income, taxed at slab rate
- File ITR-3 (business income)
- If F&O turnover > ₹10 Cr: Tax audit mandatory under Section 44AB
- F&O turnover calculation: Sum of absolute values of profit + loss on all trades
- If turnover < ₹2 Cr and profit > 6% of turnover: Can file under Section 44AD (presumptive)
- F&O losses can offset any income except salary
- Can carry forward F&O losses for 8 years
- STT on options: 0.1% on sell side premium (increased in Budget 2024)
- STT on futures: 0.0125% on sell side

SEBI F&O RULES (2024-25):
- Weekly expiry rationalization: Only one benchmark index per exchange for weekly expiry
- Upfront premium collection: Option buyers must pay full premium upfront
- Minimum contract size: Increased from ₹5L to ₹15L for new contracts
- Position limits: Exchange-defined limits for individuals and institutions
- SEBI Study: 9 out of 10 individual F&O traders incur losses
- Average loss per trader: ₹1.2 lakh per year (including transaction costs)

KEY OPTION CHAIN METRICS:
- Put-Call Ratio (PCR): Total put OI / Total call OI. PCR > 1 = bullish, PCR < 1 = bearish
- Max Pain: Strike price where maximum options expire worthless. Market tends to gravitate here.
- Open Interest (OI): Number of outstanding contracts. High OI at strike = strong support/resistance
- IV (Implied Volatility): Market's expectation of future volatility. High IV = expensive options.
- IV Rank/Percentile: Where current IV stands relative to past 1-year range.
