INDIAN INCOME TAX RULES — FY 2024-25 (AY 2025-26)
====================================================

NEW TAX REGIME (DEFAULT FROM FY 2023-24 ONWARDS)
-------------------------------------------------
Income Slab (₹)            Tax Rate
Up to 3,00,000             Nil
3,00,001 to 7,00,000       5%
7,00,001 to 10,00,000      10%
10,00,001 to 12,00,000     15%
12,00,001 to 15,00,000     20%
Above 15,00,000            30%

Standard deduction: ₹75,000 for salaried individuals under new regime (Budget 2024).
Rebate under Section 87A: Full rebate for taxable income up to ₹7,00,000.

OLD TAX REGIME (OPTIONAL, MUST OPT-IN)
---------------------------------------
Income Slab (₹)            Tax Rate
Up to 2,50,000             Nil
2,50,001 to 5,00,000       5%
5,00,001 to 10,00,000      20%
Above 10,00,000            30%

Old regime allows deductions under 80C, 80D, HRA, LTA, etc.
Standard deduction: ₹50,000 under old regime.

CAPITAL GAINS TAX ON EQUITY (POST JULY 2024 BUDGET CHANGES)
============================================================

SHORT TERM CAPITAL GAINS (STCG) ON EQUITY:
- Holding period: Less than 12 months
- Tax rate: 20% flat (increased from 15% in Budget 2024)
- Applies to listed equity shares and equity mutual funds
- STT (Securities Transaction Tax) must have been paid at time of purchase/sale

LONG TERM CAPITAL GAINS (LTCG) ON EQUITY:
- Holding period: 12 months or more
- Tax rate: 12.5% above ₹1.25 lakh annual exemption (changed from 10% above ₹1 lakh)
- Applies to listed equity shares and equity mutual funds
- No indexation benefit for equity
- Annual exemption: First ₹1,25,000 of LTCG is tax-free

CAPITAL GAINS ON DEBT FUNDS (POST APRIL 2023 CHANGES):
- STCG on debt funds: Added to income, taxed at slab rate
- LTCG on debt funds: Also taxed at slab rate (indexation benefit removed from April 2023)
- This applies to all debt mutual funds, gold funds, and international funds purchased after April 1, 2023

SECTION 80C DEDUCTIONS (OLD REGIME ONLY)
=========================================
Maximum deduction: ₹1,50,000 per financial year

Eligible instruments:
1. ELSS (Equity Linked Savings Scheme) — 3-year lock-in, market-linked
2. PPF (Public Provident Fund) — 15-year lock-in, currently 7.1% p.a., tax-free maturity
3. LIC Premium — life insurance premiums
4. NPS (National Pension System) — Tier 1 contributions
5. ULIP (Unit Linked Insurance Plan) — insurance + investment combo
6. NSC (National Savings Certificate) — 5-year lock-in, fixed interest
7. Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana — for girl child, high interest
8. Home loan principal repayment
9. 5-year Fixed Deposit (tax saving FD)
10. Tuition fees for children (up to 2 children)
11. EPF (Employee Provident Fund) — employer deducts from salary

SECTION 80CCD(1B) — ADDITIONAL NPS DEDUCTION
=============================================
Additional ₹50,000 deduction over and above 80C limit.
Available in both old and new tax regimes (new regime allows only this and standard deduction).
Total NPS benefit: ₹1,50,000 (under 80C) + ₹50,000 (under 80CCD(1B)) = ₹2,00,000.

SECTION 80D — HEALTH INSURANCE DEDUCTION
==========================================
For self/spouse/children:
- Up to ₹25,000 for individuals below 60 years
- Up to ₹50,000 for senior citizens (60+ years)

For parents:
- Additional ₹25,000 (below 60) or ₹50,000 (senior citizen)

Maximum total: ₹1,00,000 (if both self and parents are senior citizens)
Preventive health check-up: ₹5,000 included within above limits.

TAX-LOSS HARVESTING
====================
- Book unrealized losses before March 31 each year to offset against capital gains
- STCL (Short Term Capital Loss) can offset both STCG and LTCG
- LTCL (Long Term Capital Loss) can only offset LTCG
- Unabsorbed losses can be carried forward for 8 assessment years
- Wash sale rule: No specific rule in India, but transactions must be genuine
- Strategy: Sell losing positions, book loss, re-enter after settlement if still bullish

SECURITIES TRANSACTION TAX (STT)
=================================
- Equity delivery buy: 0.1% of transaction value
- Equity delivery sell: 0.1% of transaction value
- Intraday sell: 0.025% of transaction value
- Futures sell: 0.0125% of transaction value
- Options sell (on premium): 0.1% of premium (increased in Budget 2024)
- STT is not a separate deductible; it is part of cost of acquisition

SURCHARGE ON INCOME TAX
========================
- Income above ₹50 lakh: 10% surcharge
- Income above ₹1 crore: 15% surcharge
- Income above ₹2 crore: 25% surcharge
- Income above ₹5 crore: 37% surcharge (capped at 25% for capital gains)
- Health and Education Cess: 4% on tax + surcharge
