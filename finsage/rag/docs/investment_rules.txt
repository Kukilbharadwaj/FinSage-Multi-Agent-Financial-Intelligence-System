INDIAN PERSONAL FINANCE AND INVESTMENT RULES
==============================================

THE 50-30-20 BUDGETING RULE (ADAPTED FOR INDIA)
-------------------------------------------------
This is the most popular budgeting framework for Indian salaried individuals:

50% — NEEDS (Essential Expenses):
- Rent or EMI payment
- Groceries and household items
- Utilities (electricity, water, gas, internet)
- Transportation (fuel, metro pass)
- Insurance premiums (term life, health)
- Loan EMIs (education, personal, car)
- Children's school fees

30% — WANTS (Lifestyle & Discretionary):
- Dining out, food delivery (Zomato, Swiggy)
- Entertainment (movies, OTT subscriptions)
- Shopping (clothes, gadgets)
- Vacations and travel
- Hobbies and fitness (gym membership)
- Upgrades and luxury purchases

20% — SAVINGS & INVESTMENTS:
- Emergency fund contribution
- SIP in mutual funds
- PPF / NPS contributions
- ELSS for tax saving
- Fixed deposits
- Stock investments
- Gold (digital or SGB)

For lower salary brackets (below ₹25,000/month), adjust to 60-20-20 or even 70-10-20.
For higher salary brackets (above ₹1,00,000/month), target 40-30-30 or higher savings rate.

EMERGENCY FUND
===============
Target: 3 to 6 months of essential monthly expenses.
Where to keep: Liquid mutual fund or high-interest savings account (NOT fixed deposit).
Why liquid fund: Instant redemption up to ₹50,000, returns better than savings account.
When to use: Job loss, medical emergency, major unexpected repair.
Rule: Build emergency fund BEFORE starting any other investment.
For freelancers/self-employed: Target 6 to 12 months of expenses.

SIP (SYSTEMATIC INVESTMENT PLAN) BASICS
=========================================
What: Fixed amount invested monthly into a mutual fund automatically.
How it works: Rupee cost averaging — buy more units when market is low, fewer when high.
Why SIP beats lump sum: Removes timing risk. Most retail investors cannot time the market.
Minimum SIP: As low as ₹500/month in many funds.
Best for: Long-term wealth creation (5+ years).
SIP in index fund vs active fund: Index funds have lower expense ratio (0.1-0.3% vs 1-2%).
Popular index funds: Nifty 50 index fund, Nifty Next 50, S&P 500 (international).
Rule: Never stop SIP during market crashes — that's when you get the best prices.

INDEX FUND VS ACTIVE FUND FOR INDIAN RETAIL INVESTORS
======================================================
Index Fund advantages:
- Lower expense ratio (0.1% to 0.5%)
- No fund manager risk
- Tracks benchmark (Nifty 50, Sensex)
- Most active funds fail to beat index over 10+ years
- Ideal for beginners

Active Fund advantages:
- Can outperform in certain market conditions
- Better for mid-cap and small-cap segments
- Fund manager expertise in stock selection

Recommendation for beginners: Start with Nifty 50 index fund SIP.
As portfolio grows: Add Nifty Next 50 and one flexi-cap fund.

PPF (PUBLIC PROVIDENT FUND)
============================
Current interest rate: 7.1% per annum (reviewed quarterly by government)
Lock-in period: 15 years (partial withdrawal from year 7)
Tax treatment: EEE (Exempt-Exempt-Exempt) — contribution, interest, maturity all tax-free
Maximum annual contribution: ₹1,50,000
Minimum annual contribution: ₹500
Tax benefit: Under Section 80C (up to ₹1,50,000)
Best for: Risk-averse investors, guaranteed returns, long-term goal planning
Compounding: Annual compounding, interest calculated monthly

NPS (NATIONAL PENSION SYSTEM)
==============================
Tax benefit: ₹1,50,000 under 80C + additional ₹50,000 under 80CCD(1B) = ₹2,00,000 total
Lock-in: Until age 60 (early exit has restrictions)
At maturity: 60% lump sum (tax-free), 40% must buy annuity (taxable as income)
Asset allocation: Equity (up to 75%), Corporate bonds, Government securities
Expense ratio: Extremely low (0.01% to 0.09%)
Best for: Additional tax saving beyond 80C, retirement planning
Note: Less liquid than mutual funds, but excellent for retirement corpus

ELSS (EQUITY LINKED SAVINGS SCHEME)
=====================================
Lock-in period: 3 years (shortest among 80C options)
Returns: Market-linked, typically 10-15% CAGR over long term
Tax benefit: Under Section 80C (up to ₹1,50,000)
Tax on gains: LTCG at 12.5% above ₹1.25 lakh
Best for: Tax saving with equity exposure, SIP mode recommended
Popular ELSS funds: Mirae Asset Tax Saver, Axis Long Term Equity, Quant ELSS

FIXED DEPOSIT VS DEBT MUTUAL FUND
====================================
Fixed Deposit:
- Guaranteed returns (6-7.5% currently)
- Interest taxable at slab rate every year (TDS applies)
- Premature withdrawal penalty
- DICGC insurance up to ₹5 lakh per bank

Debt Mutual Fund:
- Returns: 6-8% (depending on fund type)
- Tax: At slab rate for both STCG and LTCG (post April 2023)
- More liquid — redemption in 1-3 days
- No TDS (tax paid at time of redemption)
- Better for higher tax brackets due to deferred taxation

INSURANCE FIRST RULE
=====================
Rule: Always get term insurance and health insurance BEFORE starting investments.

Term Insurance:
- Pure protection, no investment component
- Coverage: 10-15x annual income
- Premium: ₹500-800/month for ₹1 crore cover (age 25-30)
- Buy online for lowest premium
- Do NOT buy ULIP or endowment plans — returns are poor

Health Insurance:
- Minimum ₹5 lakh cover (₹10 lakh recommended for family)
- Get super top-up for additional coverage at low cost
- Section 80D tax benefit
- Buy before any pre-existing conditions develop
- Corporate health insurance is NOT enough — get personal cover

GOLD INVESTMENT OPTIONS
========================
- Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGB): Best option — 2.5% annual interest + gold price appreciation, tax-free LTCG at maturity
- Digital Gold: Easy to buy/sell, no interest, capital gains apply
- Gold ETF: Listed on stock exchange, tracks gold price
- Physical Gold: Avoid for investment due to making charges, storage, purity concerns
